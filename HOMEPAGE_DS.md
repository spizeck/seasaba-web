# Sea Saba — Homepage Product Specification (Revised)

## Core Philosophy

Sea Saba is not simply selling dive trips.

The homepage should help visitors answer:

1. Why should I visit Saba?
2. Why should I dive with Sea Saba?
3. Why should I book now?

The website should feel like a premium adventure-travel destination, combining the storytelling of National Geographic with the trust and conversion focus of a modern travel company.

---

# Recommended Homepage Structure

## 1. Hero Section

Full-screen immersive video or photography showcasing:

* Saba's dramatic underwater pinnacles
* Healthy coral reefs
* Sharks, turtles, and rays
* Sea Saba boats in operation

### Primary CTAs

* Book Diving
* Plan Your Trip
* Explore Dive Sites

### Trust Indicators

* Since 1985
* Max 8:1 Guide Ratio
* 43+ Dive Sites
* 10–25 Minute Boat Rides

---

## 2. Why Saba

Introduce Saba as one of the Caribbean's most unique dive destinations.

### Key Themes

* No cruise ships
* No mass tourism
* Protected marine park
* Volcanic island landscape
* World-famous pinnacles
* Authentic Caribbean culture

Large photography and storytelling should dominate this section.

---

## 3. Dive Saba's World Famous Pinnacles

Dedicated section highlighting:

* Third Encounter (The Needle)
* Twilight Zone
* Outer Limits
* Mt. Michel
* Shark Shoals

Explain why these sites are internationally recognized and unique.

### CTA

* Explore All Dive Sites

---

## 4. Marine Life

Large image-driven section focused on:

* Reef Sharks
* Nurse Sharks
* Green Turtles
* Hawksbill Turtles
* Eagle Rays
* Frogfish
* Seahorses
* Lobsters
* Healthy Coral Reefs

### CTA

* Discover Marine Life

## 5. Featured Dive Sites

* Marine life

Highlight:

Feature verified reviews from TripAdvisor and Google.

Use actual review counts and ratings.

Include:

* Guest testimonials

* Awards and recognitions

### CTA

* Read More Reviews

## 8. Group Travel

Dedicated section for:

* Dive Clubs

* Tour Operators

Explain:

* Flexible scheduling

* Local expertise

### CTA

## 9. Plan Your Trip

Travel-planning hub introducing:

* Getting to Saba

* Ferry options
* Transportation
* What to pack
* Frequently asked questions

### CTA

---

## 10. Where to Stay

Accommodation recommendations and partner properties.

This section should support trip planning rather than dominate the homepage.

### CTA

* View Accommodations

---

## 11. FAQ

Address common concerns:

* How do I get to Saba?
* What certification do I need?
* When is the best time to dive?
* Can non-divers join?
* What marine life can I expect?

---

## 12. Footer

Contact information, booking links, policies, social channels, and quick navigation.

---

# Design Direction

The visual design should feel:

* Premium
* Authentic
* Adventurous
* Professional
* Photography-driven

Avoid:

* Generic resort aesthetics
* Corporate stock imagery
* Excessive animations
* Cluttered layouts

---

# Primary Conversion Goals

1. Book Diving
2. Request Group Quote
3. Plan Your Trip

All homepage sections should support one or more of these goals.
